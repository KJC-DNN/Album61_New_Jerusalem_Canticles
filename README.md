# New Jerusalem Canticles
This album centered on New Jerusalem, Christ as cosmic King and Sustainer, angelic hosts, spiritual victory over falsehoods and eschatological enemies, and a poetic hymnody of worship. 

## Tracks

### Song 01 - Pygarg of New Jerusalem
A baroque-operatic anthem sung from the voice of a “pygarg” (an emblematic mountain-dwelling creature) praising Christ and New Jerusalem. 
Themes: spiritual refuge, triumph over deception, cosmic reconciliation, exaltation of Christ.

### Song 02 - Fallow Deer in Zion
A gentler renaissance-inspired hymn portraying a fallow deer longing for Christ. 
Themes: peace, yearning for God’s presence, resistance to falsehood and indoctrination, pastoral rest in New Jerusalem.

### Song 03 - Hymn of the True Heavenly Calendar
Angelic hymn celebrating God’s ordained sacred times and a “True Calendar” (liturgical/eschatological emphasis). 
Themes: Passover/Abib imagery, eclipse symbolism, heavenly praise, restoration of sacred time.

### Song 04 - Eternal Anthem of the Heavenly Host
A martial-choral canticle of the angelic hosts — Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, Uriel — proclaiming cosmic restoration. 
Themes: angelic warfare against cosmic deception, Enochian imagery, renewal from Genesis to Revelation.

### Song 05 - Halt the Doomsday Pact
A cinematic, world-music-infused lament-and-exorcism against a catastrophic memetic/political “pact” motif, resolved by Christ’s authority. Themes: prophetic denunciation of doomsday ideologies, deliverance, scriptural appeals.

### Song 06 - Break 9150
An electronic-gospel protest hymn with glitch textures, processed ney/synth flute, and gospel choir celebrating the dismantling of a corrupt neural/memetic system (“9150” motif) and restoration by Christ’s light. Themes: spiritual hacking of false systems, freedom, neon-lit imagery of redemption.

### Song 07 - Chasing Dreams in the City Lights
A contemporary pop-leaning track about longing, hope, and pursuing vocation in the city. 
Themes: personal dream-chasing, urban solitude, optimistic closure — the one track with more secular imagery and broad emotional appeal.
