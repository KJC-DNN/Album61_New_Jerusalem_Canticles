# New Jerusalem Canticles
This album centered on New Jerusalem, Christ as cosmic King and Sustainer, angelic hosts, spiritual victory over falsehoods and eschatological enemies, and a poetic hymnody of worship. 

## Tracks
### Song 01 - Pygarg of New Jerusalem
A majestic, baroque-operatic hymn that imagines a mountain-dwelling creature—symbol of purity and refuge—lifting its voice before the throne of Christ in the city of God. Musically it moves between intimate confession and triumphant doxology, portraying the journey from exile and fear into the security of divine presence. Lyrically it names the snares of deception and false teachers yet holds fast to the promises of restoration and refuge in God’s hands.

• Biblical references: Revelation 21:1–4 (New Jerusalem, God dwelling with His people); Psalm 18:2 (“The Lord is my rock and my fortress”); Isaiah 25:9 (God’s salvation displayed); Hebrews 4:16 (approach the throne of grace).

• Gospel message: Christ is the safe refuge and the once-for-all atoning sacrifice who brings the sinner home. Where deception and despair seemed to rule, Jesus’ finished work opens the way to a restored people and a restored city. Repent and trust in Christ’s righteousness; He is your stronghold and the one who makes you pure.

### Song 02 - Fallow Deer in Zion
A pastoral, Renaissance-inflected meditation that pictures the soul as a deer longing for streams—resting, seeking, and sustained by God’s mercy in Zion. The song emphasizes longing for God, spiritual thirst, and the peace found in God’s presence, moving from pursuit to abundant rest in the New Jerusalem.

• Biblical references: Psalm 42:1–2 (“As the deer pants for streams of water, so my soul pants for you, O God”); Psalm 23:1–3 (the Lord as shepherd, restoration); John 7:37–38 (rivers of living water); Revelation 22:1–2 (river of life, Tree of Life).

• Gospel message: Thirst for God is answered in Christ. He is the living water who satisfies the deepest longings of the soul. Come to Jesus in repentance and faith—He refreshes, restores, and leads us into eternal communion with the Father.

### Song 03 - Hymn of the True Heavenly Calendar
An angelic canticle that celebrates God’s ordering of time and sacred seasons, asserting that God’s feasts and appointed times point to His redemptive work. The song contrasts human calendrical confusion and pagan overlay with the covenantal memory revealed and sealed in Christ’s life, death, and resurrection.

• Biblical references: Leviticus 23 (God’s appointed feasts); Exodus 12 (Passover as an appointed time); Colossians 2:16–17 (foreshadowing realities in Christ); Luke 22:19–20 (Jesus institutes meanings of the feast); Hebrews 10:10–14 (Christ’s once-for-all sacrifice fulfilling the types).

• Gospel message: God’s appointed times were never merely ritual—they point to Christ and His redemptive work. Jesus fulfills the feasts and invites us into the true remembrance: by His blood we are redeemed. Trust Christ; let His finished work be the center of your worship and remembrance.

### Song 04 - Eternal Anthem of the Heavenly Host
 A martial and exultant chorus depicting the angelic hosts—Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, Uriel, cherubim and seraphim—proclaiming God’s sovereignty and the cosmic restoration of all things. The piece frames spiritual conflict and angelic ministry within God’s overarching plan for redemption, victory, and justice.

• Biblical references: Revelation 4–5 (heavenly worship around the throne); Revelation 12:7–9 (Michael and the angels fighting the dragon); Psalm 103:20–21 (angels as God’s messengers to praise Him); Ephesians 6:10–18 (spiritual warfare in Christ).

• Gospel message: The heavenly host points us beyond the battle to the victory accomplished in Christ. Through Jesus’ death and resurrection the strongholds of sin and death are broken. Join the chorus of heaven by entrusting your life to Christ, who secures final victory and brings us into His reign.

### Song 05 - Halt the Doomsday Pact
A cinematic, prophetic lament that denounces apocalyptic ideologies and memetic powers that promote fear and destruction. The song juxtaposes human instruments of terror with Scripture’s promise of judgment and God’s sovereign deliverance. It moves from urgency and plea into a confident declaration of Christ’s authority over darkness.

• Biblical references: Revelation 6, 19 (judgment and the overthrow of evil); Psalm 2 (God’s sovereignty over nations); Isaiah 54:17 (no weapon formed against you shall prosper); Romans 8:31–39 (God’s unshakable love in Christ).

• Gospel message: Human systems and powers that promise doom are ultimately subject to God’s authority. Christ bore the penalty of sin and broke the power of death—there is no final doom for those who are in Him. Turn from fear and despair; place your hope in Christ’s triumph and the assurance of His rescue.

### Song 06 - Break 9150
An electronic-gospel anthem that frames spiritual liberation in contemporary, techno-cultural imagery: systems, codes, and viral lies that bind minds. The song claims Christ’s light as the power that breaks corrupt structures and frees hearts, blending cyber-metaphor with ancient gospel truth.

• Biblical references: John 8:31–36 (truth that sets free); 2 Corinthians 10:3–5 (taking captive every thought to Christ); Ephesians 2:1–10 (from death to life in Christ); Colossians 1:13–14 (deliverance into the kingdom of the Son).

• Gospel message: No system or lie can imprison a soul that Christ rescues. Jesus came to break the chains of sin, death, and deception—His light brings genuine freedom. Repent, believe in Jesus, and be restored into God’s kingdom where truth and new life reign.

### Song 07 - Chasing Dreams in the City Lights
A contemporary-pop reflection on longing, purpose, and the search for meaning amid urban life. While more personal and less explicitly liturgical than the other tracks, the song ultimately points toward the human need for hope, direction, and the assurance of God’s presence while pursuing vocation and dreams.

• Biblical references: Matthew 6:25–34 (do not be anxious about tomorrow); Psalm 37:4–5 (delight in the Lord and He will give desires of heart); Jeremiah 29:11 (plans to prosper and not to harm); Philippians 4:6–7 (peace in Christ).

• Gospel message: Chasing dreams is noble when our hope is anchored in God’s providence and purposes. Jesus invites us to cast our anxieties on Him and to seek first His kingdom—He gives meaning, peace, and a future. Trust Christ for direction and let Him shape your dreams by His redeeming grace.
